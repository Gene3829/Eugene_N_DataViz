USA vs Canada 1

Chart Images

Point 1 Paragraph

These are the comparison graphs of the number of Olympic medals won between the U.S. and Canada. Based on the total number of medals won by the two countries, the U.S. has 653 medals, Canada has 625 medals, so the U.S. won 28 more medals than Canada. However, based on the total number of gold medals won by the two countries, the U.S. has 167 and Canada has 315, showing an overwhelming difference with Canada winning 148 more medals than the U.S.

The overall winning rate shows that the U.S. is slightly better than Canada, but Canada has a much higher gold medal-winning rate than the U.S.

Point 2 Paragraph

These are the comparison graphs of the number of Olympic medals won between Japan and the Unified Team. Based on the total number of medals won by male athletes from both countries, Japan has 50 medals, the EUN has 33 medals, so Japan won 17 more medals than the EUA. However, based on the total number of medals won by female athletes from both countries, Japan has 13 and the EUN has 27, showing not much difference with the ratio of medals won by male athletes from both countries.

The proportion of medals won by men and women in both countries is not much different, so the two countries are almost tied.
